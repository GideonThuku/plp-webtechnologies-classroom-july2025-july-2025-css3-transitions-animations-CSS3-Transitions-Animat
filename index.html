About Us

Home / About Us

Africa Ability Trust is a registered nonprofit organization in Kenya, empowering Persons with Disabilities and caregivers by equipping communities, organizations and churches to build inclusive spaces of dignity and hope.

Africa Ability Trust is based in Kibera, just five kilometers southwest of Nairobi. Though it spans only 2.5 square kilometers, Kibera is home to nearly one million people, making it one of the most densely populated places in the world.

In the heart of this vibrant yet underserved community, we walk with families navigating disability, poverty, and social stigma. Our work is rooted in proximity, dignity, and lasting transformation.

What We Do

Home / What We Do

Our work is guided by five core pillars that reflect our commitment to restoring dignity, inclusion, and discipleship for persons with disabilities (PWDs) and their caregivers.

01

Family & Caregiver Empowerment

We uplift the entire caregiving unit of Persons with disabilities (PWDs), emotionally, economically, and spiritually, recognizing that empowered families create sustainable support systems.

  • Emotional support programs
  • Economic empowerment initiatives
  • Spiritual guidance and counseling
02

Holistic Community Support

We meet practical needs through therapy, training, and advocacy, while tackling stigma and systemic exclusion at the grassroots level.

  • Therapy and rehabilitation services
  • Skills training programs
  • Community advocacy initiatives
03

Faith-Inclusive Transformation

We restore dignity by integrating persons with disabilities into the spiritual life of churches and communities.

  • Church accessibility programs
  • Inclusive worship practices
  • Spiritual mentorship
04

Grassroots Impact, Scalable Models

We start local, in communities like Kibera, and develop inclusive models that are community-driven, faith-rooted, and scalable across Africa.

  • Local community programs
  • Scalable model development
  • Cross-Africa expansion
05

Church & Parachurch Equipping

We train leaders, equip churches and parachurch, and build the theological and structural capacity for disability ministry rooted in sound doctrine and love.

  • Leadership training programs
  • Theological education
  • Structural capacity building
